Cyber Awareness

🛡️ Cyber Safety Checklist
for Kids

A complete guide for children, parents & educators

🔐
ℹ️ How to use this checklist: Go through each section with your child. Check off items that are already in place. For unchecked items, make a plan to action them. Review this checklist every 3 months.
🧒

Personal Information Protection

Kids
  • I never share my full name online
    Use a nickname or username instead of your real name
  • I never share my home address or school name
    Not in profiles, chats, games, or comments
  • I never share my phone number with strangers
  • I keep my passwords private — even from friends
    Only share passwords with a parent or guardian
  • I do not post photos that show my location
    Avoid photos with school uniforms, street signs, or landmarks
🔐

Passwords & Account Security

Kids + Parents
  • All accounts have strong, unique passwords
    At least 12 characters with letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Two-factor authentication (2FA) is enabled
    On email, social media, and gaming accounts where available
  • I log out from shared devices (school computers, tablets)
  • I use a password manager or secure record
    Parents should keep a secure copy of children's account credentials
  • Passwords are changed every 6 months
📱

Social Media & Online Profiles

Kids + Parents
  • All social media profiles are set to private
  • Location sharing is turned OFF on all apps
  • Only real-life friends are approved as followers/friends
  • Direct messages from strangers are blocked/disabled
  • I think before posting — would I show this to a teacher?
    The "teacher test" is a great way to check if content is appropriate
  • I review my profile photo — it does not show personal details
⚠️

Recognizing & Reporting Threats

Kids
  • I know what cyberbullying looks like and how to report it
  • I do not click on suspicious links or pop-ups
    Especially offers that seem too good to be true
  • I know not to download apps or files without parent approval
  • If I feel uncomfortable online, I stop and tell an adult
  • I save evidence (screenshots) before reporting or blocking
  • I know the helpline: Childline 1098 (India) / NCMEC (Global)
🏠

Home & Device Safety

Parents
  • Parental controls are enabled on all devices
  • Screen time limits are set and followed
  • Devices are charged outside the bedroom at night
    Reduces exposure and encourages better sleep habits
  • Antivirus software is installed and updated
  • Home Wi-Fi uses WPA3 encryption and a strong password
  • Approved app/game list is maintained and reviewed regularly
💬

Communication & Digital Citizenship

Kids + Parents
  • We have regular conversations about online experiences
  • My child knows they won't get in trouble for reporting problems
  • I treat everyone online with respect — no mean comments
  • I understand that online actions have real-world consequences
  • We review our safety rules together every 3 months
