🔒 Personal Information Protection 0/8 Kids ▼
I never share my full real name online
Use a fun nickname or username — not your real first and last name.
Critical
I never share my home address with anyone online
Not in games, DMs, profiles, comments, or any online form that isn't a trusted adult site.
Critical
I never share my school name, grade, or class online
This can help strangers find your location or routine.
High
I keep my phone number private — even from online friends
Once shared, your number can be used to track, spam, or contact you.
High
My photos do not reveal my location or daily routine
Check for school uniforms, street signs, landmarks, or location tags in the background.
High
I do not share my daily schedule or when I'm home alone
Posting "at school till 3pm" or "home alone today" can put you at risk.
Critical
I understand that once info is posted online, it cannot be fully deleted
Even deleted posts can be screenshotted, cached, or shared before removal.
Know This
I use a nickname or avatar — not a real photo — for gaming profiles
Real photos in game lobbies can be screenshotted by strangers.
High
🔐 Passwords & Account Security 0/7 Kids + Parents ▼
All accounts use strong, unique passwords (12+ characters)
Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Example: S@fe#Onlin3_2026!
Critical
I never use my name, birthday, or pet's name as a password
These are the first things hackers and bad actors will try.
Critical
I do not share my password with anyone — including friends
Only share account credentials with a parent or trusted guardian.
Critical
Two-factor authentication (2FA) is enabled on key accounts
Email, social media, and gaming accounts — use an app like Google Authenticator where possible.
High
I always log out from shared or public devices
School computers, library devices, tablets — always sign out when done.
High
Passwords are updated every 6 months
Set a reminder. If you've been part of a data breach, change immediately.
Good Practice
A parent keeps a secure record of children's account credentials
Use a password manager or a locked physical notebook — not a sticky note on the screen!
Recommended
📱 Social Media Safety 0/8 Kids + Parents ▼
All social media profiles are set to private
Go to Settings → Privacy on Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, and all other platforms.
Critical
Location sharing and geo-tagging is turned OFF
Disable location access for social apps in your phone settings and turn off "Add Location" in posts.
Critical
I only accept follow/friend requests from people I know in real life
Common strangers' tactics: fake mutual friends, compliments, shared interests.
High
Direct messages from unknown accounts are blocked or restricted
Most platforms have a "message requests" filter — unknown DMs should stay in requests, not inbox.
High
I apply the "teacher test" before posting anything
Ask: "Would I be comfortable if my teacher, parent, or future employer saw this?"
Good Habit
I have never shared photos or videos that could embarrass someone
Sharing embarrassing content without consent is a form of cyberbullying and can be illegal.
Critical
Profile picture does not include school uniform or identifying details
A cartoon avatar or safe photo without location clues is best.
High
App permissions are reviewed — camera, mic, contacts are limited to trusted apps
Check Settings → Apps → Permissions. Many apps request more access than they need.
Recommended
🎮 Online Gaming Safety 0/7 Kids ▼
My gaming username does not reveal my real name or age
Avoid usernames like "JohnSmith2013" or "SarahAge12". Use something neutral and fun.
High
I do not share personal info in game chats or voice calls
Even with people who seem like good online friends — you cannot verify who they are.
Critical
I never meet in real life anyone I've only met through a game
If someone asks to meet up, tell a trusted adult immediately.
Critical
I ignore and report players who send inappropriate messages or gifts
Unsolicited gifts or overly friendly strangers in games are a red flag for grooming.
Critical
I never click "free V-bucks", free skins, or external game links
These are almost always phishing scams designed to steal your game account or device data.
High
In-app purchases require parent approval before confirming
Set up a PIN or require password confirmation on app stores and gaming platforms.
Recommended
I know how to mute, block, and report other players on each game I use
Know where the report button is before you need it. Each game platform has different steps.
Know This
⚠️ Recognizing & Reporting Threats 0/7 Kids ▼
I know what phishing looks like and never click suspicious links
Signs: urgency ("act now!"), too-good-to-be-true offers, strange sender addresses, poor spelling.
High
I know what cyberbullying is and how to report it
Repeated mean messages, exclusion, fake profiles, humiliating posts — all count as cyberbullying.
High
I screenshot evidence BEFORE blocking and reporting
Evidence disappears when you block someone. Screenshot first, then report, then block.
Critical
If something feels wrong online, I stop and tell a trusted adult
You will not get in trouble for reporting. Adults need to know to help you.
Critical
I do not download files or apps without a parent's knowledge
Even "free games" or PDF attachments from unknown sources can contain malware.
High
I know the warning signs of grooming (secrets, gifts, special friendship)
If an online "friend" asks you to keep your conversations secret, that is a serious warning sign.
Critical
I know the emergency helplines: Childline 1098 (India) / 0800 1111 (UK)
You can call these free, confidentially, any time — day or night. No question is too small.
Know This
🤖 AI, Deepfakes & Misinformation 0/6 Kids + Parents ▼
I know that AI can generate fake photos, videos, and voices of real people
Deepfakes look realistic. Always verify surprising or shocking content before believing or sharing it.
Know This
I verify news or viral content before sharing it with others
Check a second trusted source. Look for the original post date. Misinformation spreads fast.
High
I never send selfies or videos to people I haven't met in real life
Even seemingly harmless photos can be manipulated using AI tools and used against you.
Critical
I understand that AI chatbots can be wrong, biased, or manipulated
Don't share personal info with AI tools. Their responses are not always accurate or safe.
Awareness
If I see a deepfake or fake image of someone I know, I report it and tell an adult
Creating or sharing fake explicit images of anyone — including minors — is illegal in many countries.
Critical
I am cautious of urgent voice messages or video calls that seem "off"
AI voice cloning can mimic anyone's voice. If a "family member" calls asking for money or info, verify by calling them back directly.
High
🏠 Home & Device Safety 0/8 Parents ▼
Parental controls are enabled on all devices children use
Use built-in tools: Screen Time (iOS/Mac), Family Link (Android), Microsoft Family Safety (Windows).
High
Screen time limits are set and consistently followed
Recommended: under 2 hours of recreational screen time for children under 12.
Recommended
Devices are charged outside the bedroom at night
This improves sleep quality and reduces late-night unsupervised online access.
Good Practice
Antivirus and security software is installed and up to date
Use trusted software on all family devices. Enable automatic updates.
High
Home Wi-Fi uses WPA3 encryption and a strong unique password
Change the default router password. Don't use the router's brand name as your Wi-Fi name.
High
All apps and games are approved by a parent before installation
Review age ratings, requested permissions, and in-app purchase settings before approving.
Recommended
Operating systems and apps are kept updated
Updates contain security patches. Enable auto-update on all family devices.
High
Safe Search is enabled on all browsers and search engines
Enable Google SafeSearch, YouTube Restricted Mode, and use kid-friendly browsers like Kiddle for young children.
High
💬 Communication & Digital Citizenship 0/7 Kids + Parents ▼
We have regular, open conversations about online experiences
Ask open questions: "What games are you playing? Who do you talk to?" — not just "Are you being safe?"
High
My child knows they will not be punished for reporting something wrong
Fear of punishment is the #1 reason kids don't report online incidents to parents.
Critical
I treat everyone online with the same respect as in real life
Online words cause real hurt. The "digital distance" does not reduce the impact of unkind words.
High
I understand that online actions have real-world legal consequences
Cyberbullying, sharing intimate images, hacking, and online harassment can lead to legal action — even for minors.
Know This
We have agreed on a Family Digital Safety Agreement
A simple signed agreement covers: screen time, approved apps, where devices are used, what to do if something goes wrong.
Recommended
I practice healthy screen habits — breaks, posture, and sleep boundaries
Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
Wellbeing
We review this checklist together every 3 months
The digital threat landscape changes. What's safe today may not be safe tomorrow. Review regularly.
