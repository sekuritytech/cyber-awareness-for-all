Module 2 of 5

🔒 Protecting Personal Information

Your personal info is like treasure — once you give it away, you can't get it back.

🚫

Never Share Online

Full name, home address, school name, phone number, or daily routine with people you haven't met in real life.

📸

Photos Can Reveal Info

Photos contain hidden location data. Uniforms, street signs, or landmarks can reveal your school or home.

🎭

Use a Username

Use a fun nickname online instead of your real name. Don't include your birth year in your username.

Quiz: Which of the following is SAFE to share publicly online?
You're setting up a new gaming profile and filling in your bio.
A
Your home address so friends can visit
B
Your full name and birthday
C
Your favourite game and a fun nickname
D
Your school name and class
✅ Correct! A fun nickname and hobby are safe — no personal identifiers. Never share real names, addresses, or schools on public profiles.
❌ Not quite. Sharing personal details like your real name, address, or school online can allow strangers to find you. Only share a fun nickname and safe details.

⚠️ Recognizing Online Dangers

Learn to spot the warning signs before they become a problem.

👆 Click each scenario to reveal what's happening and what to do:

Danger The "Free Gift" Message
You get a DM: "Congrats! You've won a £100 Amazon voucher. Click here to claim now and verify your name and address."
Click to reveal what this is ↓
🎣 This is a PHISHING scam. Scammers send fake prize messages to trick you into giving personal information or clicking harmful links. Never click — tell an adult and report it.
Danger The "New Friend" Who Asks Too Much
A stranger online says they're 14, likes the same games as you, and after a week asks for your photo and phone number. They say "don't tell your parents."
Click to reveal what this is ↓
😈 This is GROOMING. Adults sometimes pretend to be kids to build trust. If someone asks for photos or says "keep this secret" — stop talking to them immediately and tell a trusted adult.
Warning The Viral Game Download
You see a post: "Download this FREE version of [popular game] — link in bio!" The site looks a bit different from the real one.
Click to reveal what this is ↓
🦠 This is likely MALWARE. Unofficial downloads often contain viruses or spyware. Only download apps from official stores (App Store, Google Play, official websites). When in doubt, ask a parent.
Safe Situation A Friend's Message
Your school friend texts you directly: "Hey! Did you see what happened in the game last night? 😂 Let's play tomorrow?"
Click to reveal ↓
✅ This is a normal, safe message from someone you know in real life. Communicating with verified friends about shared activities is completely fine!
Module 4 of 5

💬 Cyberbullying & Kindness Online

What you type has real impact. Learn to recognize, handle, and prevent cyberbullying.

🔄

What Is Cyberbullying?

Repeated harmful behavior online — mean comments, sharing embarrassing content, excluding someone, or spreading rumors.

📸

Screenshots First

Before blocking or reporting, screenshot the evidence. This helps adults and platforms take action.

🚫

Block & Report

Block the person on the platform and use the "report" button. You are not alone — tell an adult.

Quiz: Your classmate posts a humiliating photo of you without permission. What's the BEST first step?
You see the post on Instagram and feel hurt and angry.
A
Post something mean about them in return
B
Ignore it and hope it goes away
C
Screenshot it, then tell a trusted adult and report the post
D
Ask all your friends to report it without telling an adult
✅ Correct! Screenshot → Report → Tell an adult. This is the safest and most effective response. You deserve support — don't deal with it alone.
❌ Not the best choice. Retaliation can make things worse. Ignoring gives it more power. Reporting with friends helps but adults need to know too. Always: Screenshot → Report → Tell an adult.
