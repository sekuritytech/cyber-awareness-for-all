Types of Cyberbullying

  • Sending threatening or hateful messages
  • Sharing private photos/videos without consent
  • Creating fake profiles to impersonate
  • Publicly excluding or humiliating online
  • Doxxing — sharing personal information maliciously
  • Screenshot shaming & viral mocking

🛡️ What Parents Can Do

  • Keep communication open — no judgment zone
  • Don't immediately take away devices (they'll stop sharing)
  • Document evidence: screenshots, dates, accounts
  • Report to platform AND school authorities
  • Contact Kerala Cyberdome or dial cybercrime helpline 1930

💡 Remember: Unlike physical bullying, cyberbullying has no safe zone. It enters your home through every screen.

06 / ONLINE GROOMING
The Most Dangerous Threat
Online Grooming — How Predators Operate

Grooming is a calculated, gradual process. Understanding the stages helps you spot warning signs early.

1
🎯

Target Selection

Identifies vulnerable children via social media profiles, gaming, or chat apps

2
🤝

Friendship Building

Poses as a peer. Offers gifts, attention, games, understanding. Gains trust

3
🔒

Isolation

Gradually separates the child from parents and friends. "They don't understand you"

4
🚨

Desensitization

Introduces sexual topics gradually, normalizes boundary violations

5
⚠️

Exploitation

Coerces the child into sending images or meeting in person. Uses threats

🚩 RED FLAGS FOR PARENTS
  • Secretive about online friends
  • New gifts from unknown sources
  • Switches screens when you approach
  • Becomes withdrawn or anxious
⚠️ GROOMER PHRASES
  • "Don't tell your parents about us"
  • "You're so mature for your age"
  • "I'll send you [gift/money] if..."
  • "This is our special secret"
✅ TEACH YOUR CHILD
  • Adults never ask children for secrets
  • Say NO without guilt, ever
  • Always come to you, no judgment
  • Block and report strangers

08 / PARENT'S ROLE
The Foundation of Digital Safety
Your Role as a Digital Parent

You don't need to be tech-savvy. You need to be present, curious, and approachable.

01
🧭

Be the Guide, Not the Guard

Navigate the digital world together rather than policing from outside. Explore apps and platforms your kids use.

02
💬

Create Open Dialogue

Make home a safe place to discuss anything they encounter online — without fear of punishment or judgment.

03
📋

Set Clear Boundaries

Agreed-upon rules about when, where, and how long devices are used. Involve the child in making the rules.

04
👁️

Monitor Thoughtfully

Age-appropriate oversight — transparency over surveillance. Know who they talk to and what apps they use.

05
🎓

Educate Continuously

Digital literacy is as important as road safety. Teach privacy, critical thinking, and recognizing manipulation.

06
🤝

Model Good Behaviour

Children imitate parents. Your own phone habits, online behaviour, and digital etiquette set the standard.

09 / COMMUNICATION
The Most Powerful Tool You Have
Starting the Conversation

✅ DO SAY

💬 "Tell me about your online friends — what do you guys do together?"
💬 "If someone online ever makes you feel uncomfortable, you can always tell me — I won't be angry."
💬 "Show me what's popular on your game/app right now?"
💬 "I saw this news about online safety — what do you think?"
💬 "You're getting older — let's talk about what's okay and not okay online together."

❌ AVOID

🚫 "You're spending too much time on that rubbish again!"
🚫 "I'm taking your phone away if you do that one more time."
🚫 "Why can't you just stay offline like we did?"
🚫 "I know everything about what you do online."
🚫 Dismissing or minimizing concerns they share with you.
💡

The Golden Rule: Children who feel they can talk to parents are 4x more likely to report online incidents and ask for help before situations escalate.

10 / FAMILY AGREEMENT
Set the Ground Rules Together
The Family Digital Agreement

A simple agreement — co-created with your child — is more powerful than any parental control software.

🏠 Our Family's Digital Promise
01
Screen-free meals & mornings — We put devices away during family meals and for the first 30 minutes after waking up
02
No devices in bedrooms at night — All devices are charged in the living room after 9:00 PM
03
No talking to strangers online — We never share personal information, photos, or meet anyone we only know from the internet
04
Open about apps & friends — Kids can show parents any app or game. Parents won't overreact to honest conversations
05
Think before you post — We ask ourselves: Would I be comfortable if my family saw this? Is this kind? Could it hurt anyone?
06
Block, tell, don't retaliate — If someone is unkind online, we block, screenshot, and tell a trusted adult immediately
07
Parents model what they preach — Parents follow the same rules they set for children. No double standards
08
New apps need approval — Kids ask before installing any new app. Parents check age ratings and privacy settings together
11 / PRIVACY
Teaching the Most Valuable Lesson
Privacy & Personal Information

🔴 NEVER Share Online

  • Full name and date of birth
  • Home address or school name
  • Phone number or email
  • Photos in school uniform
  • Location tags or check-ins
  • Parents' workplace information
  • Financial information (UPI, cards)
  • Passwords — even with best friends

💡 Teach This Rule: "Post only what you'd put on a billboard in your street" — Once online, always online. Nothing truly disappears.

✅ Generally Safe to Share

  • Nickname or username (not real name)
  • General interests and hobbies
  • Public creative work with parent approval
  • School project content (no location details)

🔧 Privacy Settings Checklist

☑ Set all social accounts to Private
☑ Turn off location sharing on apps
☑ Disable GPS on photos before sharing
☑ Review who can see their friend list
☑ Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
☑ Use strong, unique passwords — use a password manager
⚠️ Digital Footprint Alert: Everything posted online creates a permanent record. A college admission board or employer may search for your child's name 10 years from now.

Know Their Platforms
Social Media Safety Guide
📘
Instagram 13+
Risks: Body image, fake filters, DM strangers, location in Stories
Set: Private account, Restrict DMs to followers only, disable "Activity Status"
🎬
Instagram Reels / Josh 13+
Risks: Viral challenges, strangers in comments, addictive short-video algorithm, geolocation
Set: Private account, restrict comments to followers, disable suggested contacts
🎮
BGMI / Fortnite 16+
Risks: Voice chat with strangers, in-app purchases, addiction mechanics
Set: Friends-only play, mute strangers, set spending limits
💬
WhatsApp 13+
Risks: Unknown group adds, image forwarding, fake news
Set: Only contacts can add to groups, hide "Last Seen", 2-step verification
▶️
YouTube 13+
Risks: Algorithm rabbit holes, comments, harmful "challenges"
Set: YouTube Kids for under 13, Restricted Mode, disable comments
🌐
Discord 13+
High Risk: Anonymous servers, NSFW content, voice chat with strangers
Action: Know their servers, disable DMs from non-friends

⚡ Key Action: Create accounts together. Learn the platform before your child does. Know their usernames. Have their passwords kept by you.

13 / WARNING SIGNS
Know When to Act
🚩 Red Flags — Act Immediately

BEHAVIOURAL CHANGES

🚩 Suddenly secretive — closes screens, takes calls in private, changes passwords
😰 Visibly upset, anxious, or withdrawn after using devices
😴 Sleep disturbances — awake late, tired during the day without explanation
📚 Sudden decline in academic performance or loss of interest in school
👥 Withdrawing from family and real-life friends
😡 Extreme anger or distress when internet access is limited

ONLINE ACTIVITY SIGNS

📱 Receiving gifts, money, game credits from someone they "know online"
🗑️ Deleting browser history, chat logs, or apps after use
👤 Has online friends they're reluctant to talk about or identify
🔞 Encounters content inappropriate for their age (violence, pornography)
📞 Receiving calls or messages at unusual hours from unknown numbers

🧭 If You Spot Red Flags

  • Stay calm — don't interrogate, have a conversation
  • Don't remove devices immediately (lose evidence)
  • Document everything — screenshots, dates, usernames
  • Contact school counselor and Kerala Cyberdome / dial 1930
